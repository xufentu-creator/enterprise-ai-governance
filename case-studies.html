Case Study 01

Enterprise Digital Transformation and Decision Efficiency Optimization

Mid-sized service organization · workflow integration · decision structure improvement

In the course of business expansion, a mid-sized service-oriented enterprise encountered problems related to fragmented systems and declining decision efficiency. Communication across departments relied heavily on manual coordination, leading to delayed execution cycles and rising management costs.

Challenge

Multiple systems lacked a unified structure, data remained disconnected, and decision-making depended too heavily on individual experience rather than stable process design.

Approach

A structured framework was introduced to align data flow, business processes, and decision paths, while adding clear judgment points at key stages of execution.

Outcome

Decision responsiveness improved, communication friction was reduced, and management gained stronger visibility across the operational system.

Case Study 02

Trust Infrastructure and Decision Architecture Design

Cross-regional digital business · AI-assisted operations · governance structure design

A digitally mediated business operating across regions introduced automation and AI-supported processes, but gradually encountered unclear accountability, ambiguous decision ownership, and weakened governance boundaries. These issues began to affect operational stability and risk control.

Challenge

Responsibility drift emerged between systems and human actors, while trust boundaries and decision ownership remained insufficiently defined.

Approach

A trust infrastructure layer and decision architecture framework were designed to clarify role boundaries, establish responsibility nodes, and improve explainability across critical processes.

Outcome

Governance structure became clearer, operational uncertainty was reduced, and the business gained a more stable basis for long-term system expansion.

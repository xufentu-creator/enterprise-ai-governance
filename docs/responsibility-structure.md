# Responsibility Structure

Responsibility structure describes how accountability is distributed across people, teams, processes, and AI-mediated systems.

As organizations adopt AI tools, responsibility can become unclear when outputs move through multiple technical and human layers.

## Purpose

This document defines responsibility structure as an applied enterprise governance concept.

It focuses on how organizations can make accountability more visible when AI systems influence decisions, workflows, and operational outcomes.

## Core Position

AI does not remove responsibility.

It changes where responsibility appears, where it becomes hidden, and where it must be clarified.

A responsible enterprise AI governance structure should make clear:

- who requests the AI output
- who interprets the output
- who decides whether to act
- who executes the action
- who reviews the result
- who responds when problems occur

## Responsibility Drift

In AI-mediated organizations, responsibility can drift when no single person or team clearly owns the final decision or consequence.

This can happen when:

- AI output is treated as neutral
- human review becomes symbolic
- teams rely on system recommendations without clear ownership
- responsibility is distributed but not documented
- escalation paths are unclear

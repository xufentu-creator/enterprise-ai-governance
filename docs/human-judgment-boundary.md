# Human Judgment Boundary

The human judgment boundary describes where human interpretation, responsibility, and contextual evaluation remain necessary within AI-mediated systems.

This boundary is especially important in complex organizations where decisions involve uncertainty, responsibility, risk, and consequences that cannot be fully reduced to data or automation.

## Purpose

This document defines the human judgment boundary as an enterprise governance concern.

It helps clarify where AI systems may support decisions, but where human judgment must remain visible, accountable, and structurally protected.

## Core Position

AI systems can process information, generate outputs, and support analysis.

However, human judgment remains necessary when decisions require:

- contextual interpretation
- ethical consideration
- organizational responsibility
- risk evaluation
- exception handling
- final accountability

## Enterprise Relevance

In enterprise environments, the human judgment boundary helps prevent responsibility from becoming hidden behind systems, dashboards, models, or automated workflows.

It supports clearer governance by asking:

- who reviews the AI-generated output
- who decides whether it should be used
- who confirms the action
- who takes responsibility for consequences
- where escalation should occur

# Decision Architecture

Decision architecture describes how decisions are structured, reviewed, escalated, and finalized within complex organizations.

In AI-mediated environments, decision architecture becomes more important because information flows faster, outputs multiply, and responsibility can become fragmented across systems, teams, and tools.

## Purpose

This document defines decision architecture as an applied enterprise governance concept.

It focuses on how organizations can make decision pathways more explicit when AI systems participate in operational, strategic, or administrative processes.

## Core Position

More information does not automatically improve decisions.

In many organizations, more information increases pressure on judgment.

Decision architecture helps clarify:

- what decision is being made
- who interprets the information
- who has authority to act
- who reviews the outcome
- who carries responsibility when consequences appear

## AI-Mediated Decision Context

When AI systems generate recommendations, summaries, rankings, or predictions, the organization still needs human judgment to determine:

- whether the output is relevant
- whether the context is complete
- whether the action is appropriate
- whether the risk is acceptable
- whether escalation is required
